ingredients:
  - 1 lb jumbo lump crab meat, picked over for shells
  - 1/3 cup mayonnaise
  - 1 large egg, beaten
  - 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  - 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  - 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  - 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  - 1/2 teaspoon Old Bay seasoning
  - 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs, divided
  - Salt and pepper to taste
  - Cooking spray
  - Lemon wedges and tartar sauce for serving
instructions:
  - In large bowl, mix mayonnaise, egg, mustard, Worcestershire, lemon juice, parsley, and Old Bay
  - Gently fold in crab meat, being careful not to break up lumps
  - Add 1/4 cup breadcrumbs and mix gently
  - Season with salt and pepper
  - Form mixture into 6 cakes, about 3/4-inch thick
  - Place remaining breadcrumbs on plate
  - Gently coat each crab cake in breadcrumbs
  - Refrigerate for 30 minutes to firm up
  - Preheat air fryer to 375°F (190°C)
  - Spray air fryer basket with cooking spray
  - Place crab cakes in basket, not touching
  - Spray tops lightly with cooking spray
  - Cook for 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden brown
  - Serve hot with lemon wedges and tartar sauce

## Tips for Perfect Crab Cakes

- **Use quality crab**: Jumbo lump is best
- **Gentle handling**: Don't break up crab lumps
- **Minimal filler**: Let crab be the star
- **Chill before cooking**: Helps cakes hold together
- **Don't flip early**: Let crust form before flipping

## Best Crab to Use

**Ranked by Quality:**
1. Fresh jumbo lump crab
2. Fresh lump crab meat
3. Pasteurized lump crab
4. Canned crab meat (in pinch)

## Homemade Tartar Sauce

Mix together:
- 1 cup mayonnaise
- 2 tablespoons dill pickles, minced
- 1 tablespoon capers, chopped
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- Fresh dill
