ingredients:
  - 12 empanada discs (store-bought or homemade)
  - 1 lb ground beef
  - 1 small onion, diced
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1/2 cup diced bell pepper
  - 1/4 cup diced tomatoes
  - 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  - 1 teaspoon cumin
  - 1 teaspoon paprika
  - 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  - Salt and pepper to taste
  - 1/4 cup chopped green olives (optional)
  - 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped (optional)
  - 1 egg, beaten for egg wash
  - Cooking spray
instructions:
  - In large skillet, cook ground beef until browned
  - Add onion, garlic, and bell pepper, cook until softened
  - Stir in tomatoes, tomato paste, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper
  - Cook for 5 minutes until flavors blend
  - Add olives and chopped eggs if using
  - Let filling cool completely
  - Place empanada disc on clean surface
  - Add 2-3 tablespoons filling to center
  - Brush edges with water
  - Fold disc in half and press edges to seal
  - Crimp edges with fork for decoration and better seal
  - Repeat with remaining discs
  - Preheat air fryer to 375°F (190°C)
  - Brush empanadas with beaten egg
  - Spray air fryer basket with cooking spray
  - Place empanadas in single layer
  - Cook for 10-12 minutes until golden brown
  - Serve hot with chimichurri or salsa

## Perfect Empanada Tips

- **Cool filling completely**: Prevents soggy dough
- **Seal edges well**: Use fork to crimp
- **Don't overfill**: Makes sealing difficult
- **Egg wash**: Creates beautiful golden color
- **Work in batches**: Don't overcrowd air fryer

## Homemade Chimichurri Sauce

Blend together:
- 1 cup fresh parsley
- 1/4 cup fresh cilantro
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1/3 cup olive oil
- 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
- 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
- Salt and pepper

## Make-Ahead Instructions

**To Freeze:**
1. Assemble empanadas but don't cook
2. Place on baking sheet, freeze until solid
3. Transfer to freezer bags
4. Store up to 3 months
5. Cook from frozen, adding 5 minutes to cooking time
