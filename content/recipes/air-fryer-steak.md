ingredients:
  - 2 ribeye or sirloin steaks (1-inch thick, 8-10 oz each)
  - 2 tablespoons olive oil
  - 2 teaspoons coarse salt
  - 1 teaspoon black pepper
  - 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  - 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  - 2 tablespoons butter
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - Fresh thyme sprigs
instructions:
  - Remove steaks from refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking
  - Pat steaks completely dry with paper towels
  - Rub both sides with olive oil
  - Season generously with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and rosemary
  - Preheat air fryer to 400°F (204°C) for 5 minutes
  - Place steaks in air fryer basket, not touching
  - For medium-rare cook 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway (internal temp 130-135°F)
  - For medium cook 12-14 minutes (internal temp 135-145°F)
  - Remove steaks and let rest for 5 minutes
  - While resting, melt butter with garlic and thyme
  - Pour garlic butter over steaks before serving

## Steak Doneness Guide

**Rare:** 120-125°F (49-52°C) - 8-10 minutes
**Medium-Rare:** 130-135°F (54-57°C) - 10-12 minutes
**Medium:** 135-145°F (57-63°C) - 12-14 minutes
**Medium-Well:** 145-155°F (63-68°C) - 14-16 minutes
**Well-Done:** 160°F+ (71°C+) - 16-18 minutes

## Pro Tips for Perfect Steak

- **Room temperature**: Let steak sit out 30 minutes before cooking
- **Dry surface**: Pat completely dry for better sear
- **Don't flip early**: Resist urge to flip before halfway point
- **Use thermometer**: Only reliable way to check doneness
- **Rest time**: Critical for juicy steak - don't skip!

## Best Cuts for Air Fryer

1. **Ribeye** - Most flavorful, well-marbled
2. **NY Strip** - Tender with good beef flavor
3. **Sirloin** - Leaner, budget-friendly
4. **Filet Mignon** - Most tender, mild flavor
